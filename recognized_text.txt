Tigers, like humans, have various traditions based on their species and cultural significance. In the wild, tigers have behavioral traditions, such as teaching their cubs to hunt. Cubs follow their mothers to learn hunting skills and survival tactics. In human culture, tigers hold a prominent place. In Chinese tradition, the tiger represents courage, power, and protection; it's one of the 12 zodiac animals. In India, tigers are revered in various myths and often symbolize strength and valor. These traditions reflect the deep connection and respect humans have for tigers across different cultures and practices.
